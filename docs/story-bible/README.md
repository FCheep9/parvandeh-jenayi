# پرونده جنایی — Story Bible

This bible preserves every story concept generated for the game so any of them can be
built into a playable case later. The engine is **multi-case** by design — each case is a
self-contained data folder under `/data/cases/`, so adding a new case never touches engine
code.

- **Raw, complete forge output** (all data, machine-readable): [`story-forge-raw-output.json`](./story-forge-raw-output.json)
- **In production now:** Case 1 — *The Last Clean Take* (full spine below)
- **Fully developed, ready to build next:** *Room 114: The Quiet Hours*, *The Salvage Room*
- **Concepts (logline only, expand on request):** 7 more, listed at the bottom

These were produced by a multi-agent "story forge": 10 original premises from distinct
creative lenses → 3-judge originality/craft/fair-play panel → adversarial cliché & fair-play
hunt → top 3 developed → synthesis. Originality was weighted heavily (the brief explicitly
rejected generic crime-drama premises).

# CASE 1 — THE LAST CLEAN TAKE  *(in production)*

> **Logline.** A celebrated audiobook narrator is found dead at her home studio the night
> before her biggest release — but the deeper Detective Inspector Mara Quinn digs, the more
> she realizes the "new" recordings playing at the memorial were made in a room that no
> longer exists, and the question stops being *who killed Della Voss* and becomes *when she
> actually died, who has been wearing her voice for eleven weeks, and which of them stood
> four metres away with headphones on while she slipped under.*

**The inversion:** whodunit → *whendunit*. The body wasn't staged warm; it was staged
*quiet*. The real crime is the **theft of a death**.

### World
Present-day rainy coastal city (Portland/Bristol register). Hub: **Saltwire Audio**, a
converted Victorian row-house boutique audiobook studio — padded vocal booth, control room
with a wall of labeled drives, a damp basement archive. Photographable real props: foam
acoustic tiles, a smart insulin pen, a CGM patch, a portable field recorder, a chest-freezer
handle, a moving dolly, contract folders, a parking ticket. Secondary locations: Aldercrest
Publishing's glass office, the Brightmoor memory-care facility (Della's mother), and a
self-storage unit rented in a dead man's name. Only "tech" is plausible 2024–2026 hardware
(voice cloning, smart insulin pens, cloud CGM).

### Protagonist & stake — DI Mara Quinn
Partially deaf in her right ear from a line-of-duty explosion; hides the hearing aid under
her hair; terrified the day her ear lies to her she'll never know. The victim, **Della Voss**,
narrated the audiobooks Mara listened to through her injury rehab — Della's voice is the
sound Mara associates with relearning to live. At the memorial Mara hears the "new" Della and
something is *wrong* — too even, breaths like a metronome. A judgment only her trained,
ashamed, asymmetric ear could make — and she can't tell if she's right or if her damaged side
is finally betraying her. **To solve it she must trust the exact faculty she's most insecure
about, and the voice she must prove is fake is the one that taught her she was still alive.**

### The full truth
Della died **eleven weeks** before her body was "discovered" — alone and accidentally
(Type-1 diabetic; dosed basal insulin for a food delivery she never reached, slid into a
five-hour hypoglycemic coma in the booth). **No murder.** But her engineer/partner **Theo
Marsh** was in the control room with the talkback mic open; the session autosave preserves 90
seconds of Della slurring *"Theo… I can't… the light's wrong"* while Theo, deep in a take,
answered *"…you good? give me five,"* and went back to work. He could have reached her in
twenty seconds. **That failure — not a crime — drives everything.**

Saltwire was insolvent, but the real engine is the contract's **"Voice Continuation Rider":**
deliver all titles *alive* → synthetic-licensing rights + a retirement/sunset clause stay
with Della's estate (her written wish: *my voice dies when I do*). Die *before* delivery →
rights revert **in perpetuity** to Aldercrest, who can mint an infinite synthetic "Della"
forever. So Theo concealed the death and used a model trained on her masters to **finish the
contracted books** (trying, in his grief-logic, to keep her voice *out* of Aldercrest's
perpetual machine and keep money flowing to her mother's care). Agent **Priya Anand**
co-signed the fraud, then froze the body in a storage unit to buy time. Eleven weeks later
Aldercrest's **Gareth Vale** — who *suspected* the recordings were synthetic and wanted the
reversion — announced a surprise memorial-premiere in 36 hours, forcing either a living Della
or a death certificate. Their play: deliver the final file, get it *accepted*, then have Della
"discovered" dead of natural causes timestamped *after* delivery, betting on a quiet
certificate with no full autopsy. Priya thawed and placed the body the night before.
**Their fatal miscalculation: the one detective who knew Della's voice by heart refused to let
it be quiet.** No one killed Della. Two people stole eleven weeks of her death — and one was
four metres away while she died.

### Suspects
- **Theo Marsh** — engineer/partner; architect of the 11-week fraud & voice-puppetry, **but
  did not cause her death** (the talkback proves he *heard* and didn't move in time). Crime:
  concealment + fraud. Sin: the twenty seconds he didn't cross. *The emotional payload.*
- **Priya Anand** — agent; froze, thawed, and staged the body (her prints alone on the
  freezer handle & dolly; hardware receipt & toll records place her there). Crime:
  desecration + obstruction. Not the fraud architect, not the cause of death. Keeps her own
  dead father's voice on a reel she can't erase.
- **Gareth Vale** — Aldercrest acquisitions; *suspected* the truth and stayed silent because
  the reversion enriches his company; scheduled the event to force it. Airtight alibi (book
  fair abroad). **Morally culpable, criminally clean** — the Lead can name it on the record.
- **Junie Okafor** — junior narrator/protégé; her innocent "pickup lines" appear in the
  synthetic seams (looks complicit) — but she's the **key witness** who first says the breaths
  sound inhuman. Used, not in on it.
- **Della Voss** — the "victim." Not a murder victim at all. The crime against her is the
  **eleven weeks her death was stolen** — voice puppeted against her wish, body warehoused,
  dying breath captured as a "take."

### Fair-play spine (every fact is discoverable before the accusation)
- **Primary lock (whodunit→whendunit):** the "new" recordings carry the **old booth's** 50Hz
  hum + HVAC rattle, but a dated **renovation invoice** proves the booth was rewired 9 weeks
  ago → the "new" books were built from pre-renovation source → Della can't have recorded them
  on their claimed dates. (Two waveforms + one invoice, fully player-checkable.) Corroborated
  by metronomic breath and a *normalized-mispronunciation* splice (the model "fixed" a word
  Della always got wrong).
- **Murder ruled OUT by evidence, not fiat:** autopsy shows no trauma/ligature/petechiae,
  clean tox; CGM shows a gradual 5-hour hypoglycemic slope (not an injection crash); smart
  insulin pen + uncollected food order = accidental dose-without-meal; access log shows she was
  alone, no second entry.
- **CGM is deliberately *gated*** behind a phone passcode the player can only derive from the
  audio archive — so it *confirms* the audio deduction rather than shortcutting it.
- **Culpability split by physical evidence:** freezer/dolly prints + hardware receipt + tolls
  = **Priya** (body); voice-model training logs + forged check-ins under Theo's login = **Theo**
  (fraud).
- **The talkback autosave** is the moral core, surfaced by examining timestamps.

### Acts
1. **THE WRONG KIND OF QUIET** *(prologue/tutorial)* — catches the case at the memorial eve;
   the body looks hours-dead but the "new" Della sounds wrong to her ruined ear. Teaches
   Evidence/People/Locations/Deduction. Ends on the first crack: post-mortem interval doesn't
   match the scene.
2. **THE ROOM THAT DOESN'T EXIST** — investigation circles Theo as a fresh homicide; audio
   forensics + the ice-crystal autopsy finding collide. Lead decision: chase homicide-of-Theo,
   or the audio anomaly the ear flagged. Ends on the reframe — recordings made in a room that
   no longer exists; Della's been dead for weeks.
3. **WHO WORE HER VOICE** — the whendunit takes over: storage unit, freezer, gated CGM, the
   rider. The talkback autosave surfaces (gut-punch); the physical split forms. Lead decision:
   enter the talkback recording as evidence (name Theo's failure) or seal it.
4. **THE LAST CLEAN TAKE** *(climax/accusation)* — 36-hour clock expiring; the Lead must
   distinguish a murder that didn't happen from a fraud that did, choose the accusation
   framing (principal/accessory split; whether to indict Gareth's silence on the record).
5. **EPILOGUE — VOICE RETIRED** — branches by accusation framing + the talkback choice: the
   fate of the perpetual rights, Della's mother's care, and whether Della's voice is finally
   allowed to fall silent as she asked.

### Ticking clock
Aldercrest's surprise memorial-premiere in ~36 hours, engineered to resolve in Gareth's favor
either way. Once the (staged-time) death certificate posts at the launch, the reversion locks
forever, the cold-storage trail degrades, and Della's wish becomes legally unrecoverable.
Mara must prove the true 11-week-old time of death and stop the certificate posting at the
staged time. Holding the scene in Act 1 spends political capital that makes later hours
harder.

### 2-player (the theme *is* the mechanic)
A single authentic voice vs. a synthesized consensus — mirrored in "two minds, one
decision-maker." Both players free-browse everything; only the **Lead (Mara)** commits binding
decisions and the accusation. The **Partner** keeps an **audio-discrepancy log**: free-listen
to any recording, flag what "sounds wrong," building the synthetic-detection thread — while
the Lead must decide whether to *trust* those flags (echoing Mara trusting her own ear). The
Partner can be **wrong** (some flagged "fakes" are genuine archival Della), teaching the Lead
not to over-trust the second mind. The binding climax — distinguishing the murder-that-didn't-
happen from the fraud-that-did, and choosing framing — is a decision one person must own with
the other's counsel ringing in their ear.

### Difficulty
- **Casual:** soft board nudges; audio tools auto-annotate suspicious breath/room-tone seams;
  passcode hint; accusation accepts the correct *core* framing even if a minor accessory
  detail is misassigned (epilogue notes it).
- **Detective (default):** no auto-links; raw waveforms/spectrograms to read yourself; derive
  the passcode unaided; accusation requires the correct principal/accessory split backed by
  specific clues — a mis-citation can be challenged at trial in the epilogue.
- **Hardcore:** no nudges/annotation; board hides clue-category labels; red herrings carry
  extra weight; the game lets you file a confident *wrong* accusation; murder must be
  affirmatively ruled out with the autopsy+CGM+access-log triad; Gareth's culpability must be
  categorized as moral (not charged) or the case is thrown out.

### Theme
*What do we owe a voice after the person is gone — and who decides when it falls silent?*
Authenticity vs. fabrication; the violation of a person after death; love that cannot let go
vs. greed that will not. To find the truth Mara must trust the damaged faculty she's ashamed
of; to honor Della she must let the voice that saved her finally stop. **Grief that refuses an
ending becomes a kind of theft, and mercy is sometimes letting the last clean take be the
last.**

### Known weaknesses to write around
Single-truth replay (mitigated by moral-choice branching, not new whodunits); the 11-week
concealment must be sold hard in dialogue (forged check-ins, synthetic dailies, a mother with
memory loss); audio-forensics is the most cognitively demanding step (difficulty scaling
addresses it); the climax leans on the talkback scene + Theo's collapse landing emotionally;
Priya leaving prints needs in-world panic/time-pressure justification.

# CASE 2 — ROOM 114: THE QUIET HOURS  *(developed, build-ready)*

> **Logline.** A retired ICU nurse dies overnight in an "AI-optimized" assisted-living home,
> ruled a peaceful natural death — until Detective Mara Osei, whose own father lives one floor
> up in the same algorithm-graded wing, proves the staffing software was deliberately tuned to
> pull every aide *away* from the dying woman for forty-seven minutes. Nobody poisoned Agnes
> Whitlock; somebody engineered her to be alone.

**The inversion:** the crime is engineered **absence**, not a sealed presence. **The murder
weapon is a work schedule.** The deeper cut: Agnes died while trying to *save* the very man
who let her die, and the machine that killed her is being certified Monday for rollout across
forty more homes.

- **Stake:** Detective Mara Osei's father Daniel (early dementia) lives in the same building,
  graded by the same algorithm; she put him there because she stopped trusting her own
  vigilance — *the killer's sin and hers are the same shape.* Daniel's wing is flagged to "hold
  green" this same weekend.
- **Truth:** night nurse **Bernard Koss** (drowning in debt for his son's cancer care) hid a
  staffing-fraud inside the CareCadence algorithm; on the death-night he used a **forged
  service account** to force Agnes's wing GREEN and inflate a trivial event elsewhere, pulling
  every aide away. Agnes (ex-ICU) had cracked it — but was giving him until Monday to come
  forward *with* her, so he'd be a protected whistleblower instead of regional director
  **Diane Pruett's** pre-chosen scapegoat. He panicked and let his only ally die. Reckless
  homicide, not premeditated murder — and he's a second victim of Diane's incentive design.
- **The trap:** Agnes's nephew **Owen** loudly pushes the seductive wrong answer — "the
  algorithm did it, sue the vendor" — which the case exists to refuse (blaming the machine
  frees both human hands and lets the killing model scale).
- **2-player:** the Partner inherits Agnes's whistleblower **notebook**, sees every anomaly,
  and — like Agnes — *cannot act*, only advise; only the Lead commits. "Observation without
  authority changed nothing for Agnes" is mechanic and thesis.
- **Theme:** accountability vs. automation — the difference between the hand, the machine that
  aims it, and our hunger to blame the machine so no hand is named.

# CASE 3 — THE SALVAGE ROOM  *(developed, build-ready)*

> **Logline.** A 100-year flood drowns a county forensic lab's evidence vault the week before
> a high-profile murder trial; when the evidence is "recovered," it convicts a man your dead
> partner swore was framed — and you realize someone rebuilt the proof from scratch to bury a
> different crime entirely.

**The inversion:** the disaster everyone treats as background is the **actual crime scene**;
the chain of custody you were hired to *defend* is the murder weapon.

- **Stake:** Internal case-integrity auditor **Nadia Reyes**; her mentor Tom drowned in that
  vault after she refused to co-sign his dissent memo that the accused was innocent. She took
  this audit to learn whether the case he died doubting is the case that killed him.
- **Truth:** veteran evidence custodian **Walter Saurin** (diverting "destroyed" firearms for
  nine years) murdered the contractor who found out, then used the flood's court-sanctioned
  evidence re-collection to **fabricate** a clean case against an innocent ex-con — and drowned
  Tom to stop him securing proof. The fabricated exhibits each cite a **secondary source that
  provably couldn't have yielded them** (an intact-sealed reference vial; a duplicate arrest-
  kit the booking county never made; a report authored under an analyst who was out of state).
- **The trap:** defense investigator **Voss** insists "the flood tainted everything, throw it
  all out" — right that the accused is innocent, wrong reason; her path frees him on a
  technicality but catches no one and never proves Tom's homicide.
- **2-player:** an epistemic split made literal — one player tracks **physical** exhibit state
  (seals, weights, mud lines), the other the **paper/digital** trail (LIMS, keycard, metadata);
  no linchpin cracks alone. The crime *is* the abuse of single sign-off authority, so making
  the Lead the sole committer re-enacts the systemic failure the story indicts.
- **Theme:** trust is the unaudited signature — the single point of trust is where corruption
  hides and the innocent are lost; the redemption is the second pair of eyes Tom was denied.

# Remaining concepts (logline only — expand on request)

4. **The Donor** — A first-year med student recognizes the embalmed cadaver on her dissection
   table as her missing neighbor — but the donation paperwork names a different, living woman.
   Someone laundered a murder victim through the legal body-donation pipeline.
5. **Ground Truth** — A photogrammetry artist who builds court-admissible 3D "digital twins"
   of crime scenes is found dead in her studio; the only complete record is the scan she made
   of her own death — and you must find which parts were modeled in.
6. **The Sixth Hive** — A celebrated urban beekeeper is found dead in a sealed cold-storage
   room, ruled accidental — but the pollen in his last jar of honey describes a place he
   supposedly never went, quietly rewriting who killed him.
7. **The Last Voice** — A forensic-audio analyst is handed an "open-and-shut" overdose — until
   she realizes the dead woman is the audiologist who, twenty years ago, gave the detective her
   own hearing; the kindest lie of her childhood was a crime.
8. **The Water Clock** — A reservoir town's safety engineer dies the morning after a controlled
   flood release; every witness saw the spillway gates open at a different time and the
   evidence says all of them are telling the truth.
9. **The Glass Lake** — A celebrity free-diver dies under a frozen lake during a record attempt
   livestreamed to 90,000 — the timestamp proves she was alive on camera at 11:58, yet the
   autopsy proves she'd been dead over an hour.
